If we went to meet sophie, we would barge straight into the Tango Motel. You’ll find that it is littered with dead Peacekeepers who were trapped by the Bazaar Survivors. After searching the motel for Sophie, you’ll reach the roof and find a radio belonging to a dead Peacekeeper with Aitor trying to get through. As you respond to Aitor, you’ll get discovered by Barney, leading to him accusing you of being a traitor.


Barney will set some Survivor goons on you, so you’ll have to fight them. After finding a gated saferoom within the motel, you’ll see Barney and Sophie wielding a crossbow on the other side of the gate. Will you say that you can help each other or will you test Sophie and say “try me”?


Deciding that you think you and Sophie can help each other will lead to yet another choice to simply confirm exactly how you want to proceed. You’ll be asked if you definitely want to help Sophie and can respond either yes or no. Choosing “yes” will side you with the Bazaar Survivors and Sophie for now, and she’ll give you the quest to take back the Water Tower from Jack and Joe.


If you’re set on trying to help with Aitor’s investigation, picking the “try me!” option during this confrontation will cause you to question Sophie and Barney about who killed Commander Lucas. While you don’t really learn anything new, Sophie says that you’re more use to her alive than dead, for now, and leaves the motel with Barney. With nothing else to go on with the Bazaar Survivors, you’re forced to head back to Aitor to update him on the investigation and he’ll give you the Water Tower quest.

Giving the transmitter to Jack Matt has the immediate effect of giving total control of the radio to the Peacekeepers, causing propaganda to be blasted out across the city. Later on, you’ll have to help Jack with boosting the signal by activating extra antennas, which will eventually lead to you locating a key character for later in the story.

If you let Juan listen in to the transmitter, it will soon start a new quest, Spy Game, as you learn about the secrets Jack Matt is hiding regarding his involvement in the chemical bombing, and you'll track the shipment of UV lamps that didn't go to the VNC tower. Now after that mess, we are going to talk about the mission called "The Breakthrough".


If you sided with the Survivors and gave Frank access to the VNC Tower transmitter during The Broadcast mission, he'll be able to to help evacuate people once the missiles are first launched at the end of the Veronika mission. This can spare him a mortal wound, but Frank's fate is still in your hands.


During the Survivor main story mission, The Breakthrough, Frank will broker a meeting between himself, Jack Matt of the Peacekeepers, and Colonel Williams of the Renegades, but a truck will ambush you and shoot Frank with a poison arrow. You'll have only seconds to decide to what to do next:

We can leave Frank and go chase after the truck and trust Jack Matt to help him. Or we can try to help Frank.



If you choose to go after the truck, a timed chase around the city will start, where you'll have to follow the truck until it to a Renegades Hideout. However, Jack will radio Aiden to inform him that Frank died.

If you choose to help Frank, Juan will suggest that Margaret, the healer, might be able to help (this will only happen if she's still alive in your game). Aiden will take the arrow to Margaret; she'll tell him to look for some herbs that grow underwater, and after getting them, Aiden will deliver them to Lawan and save Frank. She'll stay with him instead of running off to the Stronghold to try and kill Williams herself.

Now I will just explain what happens during the major choices, since that is what you are here for.


When you reach the final mission in the game, you'll be forced to confront Waltz to get the GRE Key back, and you'll learn the key is the only way to stop the missiles, but Waltz needs it to keep Mia alive. Unfortunately, confrontation is unavoidable, and in the aftermath, the key is broken.

Without a key, the only way to save the city is to attach bombs to the missiles, so they blow up before reaching the city. The problem is that the bombs need to be triggered manually, so whoever triggers them will die. TO make matters worse, if the bombs are detonated, X13 will go down with it, as will Mia's chance of survival.


Now we have 2 choices to make during this. We can either:



Let Lawan detonate the bombs / Save lawan



If you choose to save Lawan, Aiden will try to stop her so she can't detonate the bombs. You'll have a few minutes to follow the yellow marker and get to Lawan's position. Once you get there, Aiden will start turning and almost kills Lawan, but he'll manage to get her out seconds before the missiles launch. Half the population of the city will die, but Lawan will live. Mia's fate is unknown, and Aiden will soon leave the city, letting whoever else survived vie for control in his absence.


If Hakon is still alive, he will come in and take care of Mia while Aiden goes for Lawan. Afterward, he will be the one who joins Aiden when he decides to get out of the city.



If you let Lawan detonate the bombs, she'll sacrifice herself as Aiden will get out of the facility with Mia, and X13 will blow up. The city will survive, but Lawan will die. Without X13, Mia will die shortly thereafter, and Aiden will leave the city.


However, if Hakon is still alive, he will rescue Lawan at the last second, and both the city and Lawan will be saved. Mia will die a couple of hours later, and Aiden will leave the city with Lawan by his side - if Frank survived as well.
